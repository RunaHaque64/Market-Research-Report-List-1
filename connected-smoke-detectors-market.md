<p><p>The connected smoke detectors market is divided into two main types: wired and wireless. Wired smoke detectors are hardwired into a building's electrical system, offering reliable functionality and consistent power without battery replacements. However, they require professional installation. In contrast, wireless smoke detectors operate on battery power and utilize radio frequency or Wi-Fi for connectivity, allowing for easier installation and flexibility in placement. Both types enhance safety by providing real-time alerts and integration with smart home systems.</p></p>
