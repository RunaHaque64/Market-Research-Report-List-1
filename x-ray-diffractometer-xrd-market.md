<p><p>An X-Ray Diffractometer (XRD) is an analytical instrument used to study the crystallographic structure of materials by measuring the angle and intensity of diffracted X-rays. The market includes two primary types: Powder XRD, which analyzes polycrystalline samples yielding information about phase identification and crystal structure, and Single-crystal XRD, specializing in the detailed examination of individual crystal structures, providing high-resolution data on atomic arrangements. Both types are crucial in materials science, chemistry, and solid-state physics for characterizing materials.</p></p>
