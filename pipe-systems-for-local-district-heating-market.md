<p><p>Pipe systems for local and district heating comprise two primary market types: hot water heating networks and steam heating networks. Hot water heating networks utilize insulated pipes to transport heated water from central plants to residential and commercial buildings, offering efficient energy distribution with lower operational costs. Conversely, steam heating networks operate by conveying steam through pipes, allowing for rapid heating and high thermal energy transfer. Both systems serve to provide effective heating solutions in urban and rural settings.</p></p>
