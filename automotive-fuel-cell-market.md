<p><p>The automotive fuel-cell market primarily consists of two types: hydrogen and methanol fuel cells. Hydrogen fuel cells generate electricity by combining hydrogen with oxygen, producing only water as a byproduct, making them environmentally friendly. Methanol fuel cells utilize methanol as a fuel source, offering easier storage and transport but requiring reforming to extract hydrogen for electricity generation. Both fuel types are crucial for advancing zero-emission vehicles, with hydrogen favored for longer distances and methanol appealing for its accessibility and infrastructure development.</p></p>
