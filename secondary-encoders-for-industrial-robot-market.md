<p><p>In the industrial robot market, secondary encoders play a crucial role in ensuring precision and feedback control. Absolute encoders provide a unique position for each rotation, enabling consistent and accurate positioning without needing a reference point. In contrast, incremental encoders track movement through relative changes in position, making them suitable for tasks requiring fast response and continuous tracking. Both types enhance robotic efficiency, but their selection depends on specific application needs and desired accuracy levels.</p></p>
