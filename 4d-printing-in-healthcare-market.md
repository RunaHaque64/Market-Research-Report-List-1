<p><p>4D printing in healthcare leverages advanced 3D printing technologies to create dynamic, responsive materials that can change shape or properties over time. Four primary market types include Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM), which extrudes thermoplastic materials; PolyJet, known for printing high-resolution, multi-material parts; Stereolithography (SLA), which uses UV light to cure liquid resin into solid layers; and Selective Laser Sintering (SLS), which fuses powdered material layer by layer. Each technology offers unique benefits for medical applications such as prosthetics, implants, and bioengineered tissues.</p></p>
