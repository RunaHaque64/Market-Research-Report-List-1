<p><p>The Non-Contact Radar Level Transmitter market is primarily categorized into two types: Guided-Wave Radar (GWR) Level Transmitters and Non-Contact Wave Radar Level Transmitters. GWR uses electromagnetic waves along a probe to measure levels in liquids or solids, providing accurate readings even in challenging conditions. Non-Contact Radar, on the other hand, utilizes free-space radar signals to measure levels without physical contact, making it ideal for applications involving high temperatures or pressures and volatile substances. Both types offer reliability and precision in level measurement.</p></p>
