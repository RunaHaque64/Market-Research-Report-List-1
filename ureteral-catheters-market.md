<p><p>The ureteral catheters market consists of various types, primarily intermittent catheters and Foley catheters. Intermittent catheters are used for temporary drainage and are inserted as needed, making them suitable for patients requiring regular bladder emptying. Foley catheters, in contrast, are indwelling devices designed for prolonged use, secured in place with a balloon and commonly employed in surgical or critical care settings. Both types play crucial roles in managing urinary issues and improving patient comfort and outcomes.</p></p>
