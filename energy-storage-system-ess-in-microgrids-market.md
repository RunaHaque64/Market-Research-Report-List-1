<p><p>Energy Storage Systems (ESS) in microgrids can be categorized into two primary market types: Grid-Tied and Independent. Grid-Tied ESS are connected to the main electrical grid, allowing for seamless energy exchange, demand response, and support during peak loads or outages. In contrast, Independent ESS operate autonomously within a microgrid, providing reliable energy storage for off-grid applications and ensuring resilience and energy security. Both types enhance energy efficiency and facilitate the integration of renewable sources into the energy mix.</p></p>
