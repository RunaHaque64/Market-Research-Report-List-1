<p><p>Cell culture media for vaccine development can be categorized into two main types: serum-containing and serum-free media. Serum-containing media utilize components derived from animal serum, providing essential nutrients and growth factors for cell proliferation. However, they may introduce variability and ethical concerns. On the other hand, serum-free media is designed to support cell growth without animal serum, offering a more controlled environment. This type is increasingly preferred for its consistency, reduced risk of contamination, and alignment with regulatory standards.</p></p>
