<p><p>The bio-digester market encompasses two primary types: dry fermentation and wet fermentation. Dry fermentation involves processing organic waste with low moisture content, typically utilizing solid substrates like agricultural residues, which generates biogas efficiently. In contrast, wet fermentation operates with high moisture content and liquid substrates, such as manure or food waste, allowing for quicker degradation. Both methods produce renewable energy and digestate but differ in their inputs, processes, and suitability for various types of organic materials.</p></p>
